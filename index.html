The Night-Before Contract

Your rational, ambitious "Evening Self" knows what time you need to get up. Nudge lets you seal that intention, setting not just a time, but a small, pre-cognitive commitment for the morning.

Why is waking up a test of willpower?

It shouldn't be. Willpower is a finite resource, especially when you're half-conscious. Nudge replaces the high-cost decision of "getting up" with a low-stakes task: drawing a simple shape. This isn't a test; it's a gentle handshake to bring your brain online.

What gets measured, gets managed.

By solving the puzzle, you haven't just silenced an alarm; you've won. Nudge tracks your performance, turning the chore of waking up into a measurable skill. That small victory provides the psychological momentum to win the next task: getting out of bed.
