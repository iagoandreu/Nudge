Answering the Important Questions

How does Nudge work?

Nudge works in three simple steps:

  1. The night before, you set a time and choose how many patterns you'll need to draw to dismiss the alarm.
  2. When the alarm sounds, a sequence of simple shapes (like a star or triangle) is shown.
  3. You trace the shapes on the screen. This small act of focus is a "cognitive handshake" that gently brings your brain into an engaged state, making the decision to get up feel natural.
Why is there no snooze button?

Because the snooze button is a design flaw that optimizes for failure. It encourages fragmented, low-quality sleep and starts your day with a lost negotiation. We didn't forget the snooze button; we fixed the problem that made it necessary. Nudge is designed to give you a small, definitive win, creating momentum that makes snoozing feel irrelevant.

How does drawing a shape make waking up easier?

It doesn't make it physically easier; it makes it *decisionally* easier. The hardest part of waking up is the high mental cost of the decision to "get out of bed." Nudge replaces that big, difficult decision with a small, easy one: "trace this shape." By completing this simple task, you've already started a chain of action, making the next stepâ€”getting upâ€”feel like a natural continuation instead of a monumental effort.

Why does this actually stop me from hitting snooze?

By the time you've watched and traced the patterns, your brain has already transitioned from a groggy, impulsive state to a conscious, functional one. The desire to snooze has evaporated because the negotiation is over. You've already proven to yourself that you are awake and capable.
